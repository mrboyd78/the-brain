# 05 — McKinsey-Grade Advice on Growth Strategy for a Solopreneur

### 1. Executive Thesis
Growth for a solopreneur is a function of **Organic Excellence** and **Dynamic Resource Reallocation**. Based on McKinsey’s "10 Rules of Growth," outperformance is driven by **Competitive Advantage** (Rule 1) and **Exposure to Market Tailwinds** (Rule 2). Strategic growth requires shifting from "Linear Labor" to "Asset-Backed Growth" (IP, automation, networks). The most disciplined choice is to **Turbocharge the Core** (Rule 4), deriving 80% of growth from existing excellence, while using a **"Shrink to Grow"** doctrine (Rule 10) to prune low-margin distractions that dilute the founder's most scarce resource: cognitive bandwidth.

### 2. What Growth Strategy Must Actually Optimize
Growth strategy must optimize for **Return on Invested Time (ROIT)** and **Unit Economics (LTV/CAC)**.
*   **The Golden Ratio:** McKinsey identifies a **3:1 LTV/CAC ratio** as the threshold for sustainable growth. A solopreneur must optimize for a CAC payback of **<12 months**.
*   **Retention-Led Velocity:** It takes **three new customers** to replace the value of one lost customer. Strategy must optimize for **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**.

### 3. Growth-Lever Framework (The Solo Growth Pyramid)
| Tier | Strategy | McKinsey Alignment | Actionable Lever |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1: Core** | **Maximize Penetration** | Rule 4: Turbocharge Core | Upsell existing products to existing customers. |
| **Tier 2: Adjacency** | **Product/Market Fit** | Rule 5: Look Beyond Core | Offer new services where "Ownership Advantage" exists. |
| **Tier 3: Horizon** | **New Arenas** | Rule 2: Make Trend the Friend | Pivot core capabilities to high-growth industries (e.g., AI). |

### 4. Focus, Expansion, and Pruning Doctrine
*   **Focus (The "Star"):** Allocate 80% of time to subsectors where **Industry Attractiveness** and **Business Strength** intersect.
*   **Expansion:** Expand only where you have a "Better Owner" status. If you cannot provide more value than a specialized competitor, it is a distraction.
*   **Pruning (The "Shrink to Grow" Rule):** Ruthlessly divest services that fall into the "Harvest/Divest" quadrant. McKinsey research shows outperformers regularly prune 2-3% of their portfolio to reallocate resources to "Granular Growth."

### 5. Failure Modes and Warning Signs
*   **The Acquisition Trap:** Over-investing in new leads while NRR (Retention) is declining. 
*   **Complexity Bloat:** Adding offers that require new skills without a 5x return on learning time.
*   **Warning Signs:** CAC Payback > 24 months; 100% time utilization with stagnant revenue; declining Net Promoter Score.

### 6. Strategic Implications for a Solopreneur Founder
*   **Capability Building:** Adopt the **70-20-10 Learning Model** (70% on-the-job, 20% exposure, 10% education).
*   **Network Integration:** B2B-integrated MSMEs have a **40% smaller productivity gap** than B2C peers. 
*   **Dynamic Reallocation:** Success is driven by *where* you compete. Reallocate 10% of "Time Portfolio" annually to new, high-growth subsectors.

### 7. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
*   **Liquidity Risk:** Divesting a "cash cow" can create immediate crises for an individual that a corporation could weather.
*   **Specialization Risk:** Extreme specialization (Rule 4) increases vulnerability to AI-driven disruption.
*   **Uncertainty:** The "Shelf-Life" of competitive advantage is shrinking, requiring continuous **Horizon 3** experimentation.

### 8. Final Recommendations
1.  **Establish the "Golden Ratio":** Never scale acquisition until LTV/CAC is > 3:1.
2.  **Apply Rule 10 First:** Prune the bottom 20% of low-margin tasks before adding new ones.
3.  **B2B over B2C:** Prioritize B2B "Network Effects" to close the productivity gap.
4.  **Protect 70/20/10:** Dedicate 10% of time to "Future Options" to avoid disruption.
