# Onboarding Positions to Avoid in Third-Party App Ecosystems

## 1. Executive Thesis
In the high-velocity marketplaces of Shopify, Atlassian, and HubSpot, a founder must explicitly avoid the "Cognitive Burden" and "Technical Debt" positions. Occupying the "Setup-Heavy" or "Vague" territory leads to immediate uninstalls and toxic reviews. The most dangerous false positive is the "Frictionless Onboarding" that installs the app but fails to deliver a functional outcome, leading to "Silent Churn" where the user forgets they ever installed the app until they see the charge on their bill.

## 2. What the Evidence Shows
*   **The "Context Switch" Penalty:** Public complaints on the Shopify App Store frequently highlight the frustration of being redirected to an external site for setup. Apps that don't use "App Bridge" (embedded UI) suffer significantly lower activation rates (Shopify.com).
*   **The "Manual Code" Death-Knell:** Reviews on Reddit and the Shopify Community show that asking merchants to edit liquid files is a top reason for uninstalls. Modern merchants expect "One-Click" theme extensions (Reddit).
*   **The "Vague Outcome" Trap:** Apps with an installation flow that ends on a dashboard with no "Next Step" are consistently cited in HubSpot marketplace reviews as "clunky" and "confusing" (HubSpot Reviews).
*   **The "Bait-and-Switch" Paywall:** Evidence suggests that gating core functionality behind a high-tier plan *after* the user has invested 10 minutes in onboarding leads to high refund rates and 1-star reviews.

## 3. Which Onboarding Positions Should Be Explicitly Avoided

### **Position A: The "Code-Required" Gatekeeper**
*   **Description:** Requiring users to manually edit theme or system files to make the app work.
*   **Why to Avoid:** It creates "Technical Anxiety." If the user uninstalls, they are left with "ghost code" that breaks their store, leading to long-term brand damage.

### **Position B: The "Vague Dashboard" Void**
*   **Description:** An onboarding flow that installs the app but leaves the user on a blank screen with no clear "Action" button.
*   **Why to Avoid:** It forces the user to think. In marketplaces, thinking is friction. If they don't know what to do in 3 seconds, they leave.

### **Position C: The "Human-Rescue" Dependent**
*   **Description:** An app that cannot be set up without a "Book a Demo" call or manual support intervention.
*   **Why to Avoid:** It kills the "Self-Serve" momentum of a marketplace. Users in these ecosystems are looking for "Instant gratification," not a sales relationship.

### **Position D: The "Over-Communicator"**
*   **Description:** Requiring the user to configure 20+ settings (colors, fonts, API keys) before they see the app work once.
*   **Why to Avoid:** It leads to "Setup Fatigue." Founders should use "Smart Defaults" and allow for later customization.

### **Position E: The "Deceptive Frictionless" Path**
*   **Description:** An app that installs in "one click" but then requires a 30-minute data mapping or CSV import to actually function.
*   **Why to Avoid:** It builds resentment. The user feels "tricked" into the install, and the gap between expectation and reality is too wide.

## 4. Dangerous False Positives
*   **High Installation Counts:** A founder may see high installs and think onboarding is fine, while the "Retention at Day 3" is near zero because the app is "Frictionless but Useless."
*   **Completion of the Setup Wizard:** 100% completion of a setup wizard is a false positive if it doesn't result in a "Functional Outcome" (e.g., the widget is live on the store).
*   **Low Initial Support Tickets:** This often means users are just uninstalling silently rather than asking for help.

## 5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
*   **Product Discipline:** You must say "No" to features that require manual user setup. If you can't automate it, don't build it yet.
*   **Review Management:** Onboarding uninstalls are the #1 source of 1-star reviews. Fixing onboarding is the best way to improve your "Social Proof."
*   **Retention Economics:** A 5% improvement in onboarding completion can lead to a 25% increase in LTV (Lifetime Value) because it compounds over the life of the user.

## 6. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
*   **The "Power User" Exception:** For complex enterprise apps, "Friction" might be necessary to ensure the user doesn't mess up their live data. In this case, "Guided Friction" is a feature, not a bug.
*   **Platform Enforcement:** As platforms (Shopify/HubSpot) automate more of their own core, third-party apps may *have* to occupy more complex niches, making "Easy Onboarding" fundamentally harder.
*   **The "Boring" App:** An app that is "too frictionless" might be perceived as low-value. Occasionally, a small "Investment Step" increases the user's perceived value of the tool.

## 7. Final Recommendations
1.  **Ban "Manual Code":** If your app requires code edits, pivot to Theme App Extensions immediately.
2.  **Implement "Smart Defaults":** Pre-fill every setting with the most common value. Let users change them *later* after they've seen the value.
3.  **The "3-Second Rule":** Every screen in your onboarding must answer "What do I do now?" within 3 seconds.
4.  **Use "Empty State" UI:** Don't show an empty dashboard. Show a "Sample Chart" or a big "Add Your First Contact" button.
