# Sharing Mode Selection: Public, Private, Embedded, and Demonstrated

## 1. Executive Thesis
In the third-party app ecosystems of Shopify, HubSpot, and Atlassian, a "one-size-fits-all" sharing strategy is a recipe for high churn and low trust. Success depends on a surgical mapping of **product utility** to **social risk**. High-visibility results should be **Publicly Shared** to build social proof; complex or data-sensitive workflows should be **Privately Recommended** through trusted intermediaries; collaborative tools should be **Embedded** directly into the user’s workspace to drive internal virality; and technical capabilities should be **Demonstrated** via sandboxes to lower buyer friction. Founders who force public sharing on private B2B workflows create "reputation friction" that kills organic growth.

## 2. What the Evidence Shows
*   **The Power of Interactive Demos:** Apps using interactive demos (e.g., Supademo) on their marketplace listings report a significant reduction in "buyer uncertainty" and higher conversion rates (Source: Supademo, Shopify App Store).
*   **Embedded Virality Efficiency:** Shopify's "App Bridge" and HubSpot's "UI Extensions" allow apps to feel like native features, which reduces the "foreign object" bias and increases internal department-to-department referral (Source: Shopify Dev, HubSpot).
*   **Public Review Permanence:** In B2B marketplaces, 5-star ratings are the primary filter for 90% of buyers, making public sharing a high-stakes reputational environment where a single bug can have long-term growth implications (Source: G2, Capterra).
*   **Privacy-First Customization:** Shopify’s shift from "Private" to "Custom" apps highlights the platform’s move toward scoped, secure, and organization-specific sharing modes (Source: Shopify Dev).

## 3. What Should Be Publicly Shared, Privately Recommended, Embedded, or Demonstrated

### A. Publicly Shared (The "Social Proof" Layer)
*   **What:** Customer "Walls of Love," high-level ROI stats (e.g., "Saved $10k on shipping"), and verified marketplace reviews.
*   **Why:** To build category authority and surface-level trust.
*   **Mode:** Marketplace listings, LinkedIn "raw screenshots," and public case studies.

### B. Privately Recommended (The "Expert" Layer)
*   **What:** Specific automation templates, custom configurations, and "SOPs" (Standard Operating Procedures) used by agencies.
*   **Why:** To bypass "Dark Social" friction. Experts recommend these in private Slack channels or 1-on-1 consulting.
*   **Mode:** Affiliate links, "Invite-only" beta access, and agency partner portals.

### C. Embedded (The "Collaborative" Layer)
*   **What:** Dashboard widgets, collaborative editing tools, and internal "mention" features (@appname).
*   **Why:** To drive **internal virality**. If a user mentions the app's output in a Jira ticket, other team members are naturally exposed to it.
*   **Mode:** Atlassian Forge panels, HubSpot CRM cards, and Shopify Admin extensions.

### D. Demonstrated (The "Technical Proof" Layer)
*   **What:** Sandbox environments, development stores, and "Interactive Tours."
*   **Why:** To prove the tool works before it touches live production data. Essential for "Low-Embarrassment" B2B sales.
*   **Mode:** Guided sandboxes and "no-code" playgrounds.

## 4. Weak Assumptions and Sharing-Mode Mismatches
*   **Mismatch 1: Publicly sharing private data.** Attempting to get users to share their "revenue dashboards" on social media. (High embarrassment/privacy risk).
*   **Mismatch 2: Demonstrating simple features.** Over-complicating a simple tool with a "Request a Demo" gate when a public video would suffice.
*   **Mismatch 3: Privately recommending "low-stakes" tools.** Keeping a simple utility app (e.g., a "currency converter") behind a referral gate when public social proof is more effective.
*   **Mismatch 4: Embedding "noisy" apps.** Placing high-frequency notifications into the host UI (e.g., Jira), leading to "app fatigue" and uninstalls.

## 5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
*   **Map Your "Sharing Unit":** Identify the *exact* piece of data or result that is "safe" to share. If it’s not safe, move to a Private or Demonstrated mode.
*   **Audit for "Reputation Friction":** If a user shares your app, does it make them look like a hero or a spammer? Design for "Hero Status."
*   **Leverage Platform Primitives:** Use App Bridge (Shopify) or Forge (Atlassian) to ensure your "Embedded" mode feels native and high-performance.
*   **Use "Demonstration" as a Lead Magnet:** Create a "Playground" version of your app that doesn't require a platform install to lower the barrier to the first "Aha!" moment.

## 6. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
*   **Platform "Lock-in":** Embedded sharing relies heavily on platform APIs. If Shopify or Atlassian changes their UI standards, your sharing logic may break.
*   **The "Shadow IT" Risk:** Custom/Private apps can bypass security audits, leading to "silent" data risks that don't appear in public reviews.
*   **Demo-to-Production Gap:** A product may look great in "Demonstration Mode" but fail in the complexity of a real production environment, leading to buyer regret.
*   **Over-reliance on Dark Social:** If 90% of your growth is "Private Recommendation," you may struggle to build the public brand needed to win the "Mainstream Majority."

## 7. Final Recommendations
1.  **Prioritize "Embedded" for Internal Spread:** Focus on how the app appears *inside* the team’s current workflow (e.g., Jira panels) to drive organic seat expansion.
2.  **Build a "Public" Wall of Love:** Curate the best "Private" praise into "Public" assets (with permission) to bridge the trust gap.
3.  **Use "Sandboxes" for Enterprise:** If targeting HubSpot/Atlassian Enterprise users, a sandbox demonstration mode is not optional—it is a requirement for trust.
4.  **Neutralize the "Powered by" Link:** If using embedded branding (e.g., "Powered by [App]"), ensure it links to a high-value "How-to" page rather than just a sales page.

---
### Internal Adversarial Review
1. **Platform Bias:** This framework assumes the platform (Shopify/HubSpot) provides good "embedded" tools. If the platform's UI is clunky, "Embedded" mode might actually *hurt* growth.
2. **Ignoring "Word-of-Mouth" vs. "Referral":** I treat them as similar, but word-of-mouth (organic talk) often follows different rules than "Referrals" (incentivized links).
3. **Data Privacy Overload:** I may be over-weighting "Privacy Risk." Some B2B users are surprisingly willing to share data if it gets them social "likes" or "expert" status.
4. **Complexity of Demo Environments:** Building a high-quality "Demonstration" sandbox is technically expensive for a small founder. I should have noted the "Build vs. Buy" trade-off for demo tools.
5. **Assumption of "Rational" Users:** I assume users choose sharing modes based on risk. Often, they just use whatever button is most visible, regardless of the consequences.
