# Ideal Customer Profile Strategy, Research, and ICP Design Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building an **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) operating system** that helps a SaaS founder, app builder, or solopreneur identify the customers most likely to buy, adopt, retain, expand, and generate healthy long-term value.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **SaaS solopreneur, founder, operator, app developer, or product business** that wants to understand:
- what an ICP actually is,
- how to distinguish an ICP from a persona,
- how to identify the companies most likely to buy and succeed,
- how to understand buyer roles and buying committees,
- how to map firmographic, technographic, behavioral, and economic fit,
- how to define exclusion criteria and negative ICPs,
- how to use an ICP to improve targeting, messaging, channel selection, conversion, retention, and expansion,
- and how to keep the ICP current as the business evolves.

The goal is to avoid a common founder mistake:

**building a vague customer profile based on intuition, broad demographics, or aspirational market size, instead of building a selection system for identifying the accounts most likely to convert, retain, and create durable profit.**

These prompts are built around real ICP research themes such as:
- best-customer analysis,
- firmographic fit,
- technographic fit,
- workflow and use-case fit,
- jobs to be done,
- pain severity,
- buying committee structure,
- trigger events,
- willingness to pay,
- retention and expansion fit,
- negative-fit signals,
- and channel and messaging alignment.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- confusing an ICP with a generic persona
- choosing customers based on market size rather than revenue quality
- ignoring technographic, operational, or implementation fit
- describing pain too vaguely to predict buying behavior
- failing to model buying committees and internal decision dynamics
- selecting channels and messages without real trust-source evidence
- accepting bad-fit revenue because negative ICP logic was never defined
- building an ICP that cannot be used for scoring, targeting, or prioritization
- assuming buyers who can use the product are buyers who will buy and stay
- creating an ICP that sounds sophisticated but does not improve GTM precision, retention, or long-term value

The goal is to force clarity on how a founder should build, score, update, and use an Ideal Customer Profile before targeting, messaging, and growth investments drift toward the wrong customers.
