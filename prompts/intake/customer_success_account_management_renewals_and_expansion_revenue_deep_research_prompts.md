# Customer Success, Account Management, Renewals, and Expansion Revenue Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building a **post-sale revenue and customer-success operating system** that protects renewals, improves adoption, expands account value, and turns customer relationships into durable growth.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, solopreneur, SaaS builder, operator, or product business** that wants to understand:
- how customer success differs from support,
- how to design post-sale account management,
- how to improve product adoption and customer outcomes,
- how to reduce renewal risk early,
- how to structure expansion motions without becoming pushy,
- how to create customer-health systems,
- how to manage strategic accounts,
- and how to make post-sale excellence a real revenue engine.

The goal is to avoid a common founder mistake:

**winning customers successfully, then treating renewals, adoption, and expansion as passive outcomes rather than actively managed systems that determine lifetime value.**

These prompts are built around real post-sale operating themes such as:
- customer success,
- account management,
- onboarding-to-value continuity,
- adoption and usage depth,
- customer health,
- renewal readiness,
- churn prevention,
- upsell and cross-sell timing,
- expansion revenue,
- executive relationship quality,
- and post-sale insight loops.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- treating renewals as calendar events instead of as managed outcomes
- letting adoption weakness quietly become churn
- expanding accounts before customer value is secure
- confusing support, success, onboarding, and account management ownership
- failing to build usable customer-health and renewal-risk systems
- allocating account-management effort without regard to value density or risk
- tracking flattering retention metrics without understanding underlying drivers
- letting post-sale insight die instead of improving product and GTM
- treating post-sale as a cost center rather than a revenue engine
- building a business that can acquire customers but cannot systematically retain and grow them

The goal is to force clarity on how a founder should design, measure, improve, and defend customer success, renewals, and expansion before weak post-sale execution undermines retention, NRR, and long-term value.
