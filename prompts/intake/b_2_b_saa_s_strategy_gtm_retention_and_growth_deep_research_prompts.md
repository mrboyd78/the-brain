# B2B SaaS Strategy, GTM, Retention, and Growth Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building and operating a **high-quality B2B SaaS business** with serious strategic, commercial, operational, and financial rigor.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, solopreneur, operator, or product builder** who wants to understand:
- how B2B SaaS businesses actually create durable value,
- how to choose the right market and ICP,
- how to design the right offer and pricing model,
- how to build a strong go-to-market system,
- how to improve onboarding, activation, retention, and expansion,
- how to manage SaaS metrics with discipline,
- how to decide between product-led, sales-led, and hybrid motions,
- and how to build a B2B SaaS company that is profitable, defensible, and hard to displace.

The goal is to avoid a common founder mistake:

**building a B2B SaaS product around vague demand, weak positioning, poor retention, expensive acquisition, and benchmark theater instead of strong unit economics and repeatable customer value.**

These prompts are built around real B2B SaaS operating themes such as:
- ARR and revenue quality,
- ICP clarity,
- GTM model selection,
- activation and onboarding,
- retention and NRR,
- pricing and packaging,
- CAC efficiency and payback,
- product-led versus sales-led design,
- customer success and expansion,
- and strategic defensibility.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- choosing an ICP that is too broad, too weak, or too hard to reach
- forcing the wrong GTM motion onto the product
- pricing and packaging in ways that hurt conversion, retention, or expansion
- treating onboarding as setup instead of as a retention lever
- chasing acquisition while retention and NRR remain weak
- overfunding GTM before growth efficiency exists
- tracking SaaS benchmarks performatively instead of using them for decisions
- claiming moats that are not actually defensible in B2B software
- building a B2B SaaS company around revenue optics instead of revenue quality
- confusing startup activity with a real, repeatable SaaS operating system

The goal is to force clarity on how a founder should build, price, sell, retain, expand, measure, and defend a real B2B SaaS business before growth turns weak economics into expensive problems.
