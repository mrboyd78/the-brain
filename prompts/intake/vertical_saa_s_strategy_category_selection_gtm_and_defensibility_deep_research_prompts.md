# Vertical SaaS Strategy, Category Selection, GTM, and Defensibility Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building and operating a **high-quality vertical SaaS business** with serious strategic, commercial, operational, and defensibility rigor.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, solopreneur, operator, or product builder** who wants to understand:
- how vertical SaaS businesses actually win,
- how to choose the right industry and niche,
- how to design software around industry-specific workflows,
- how to evaluate market size versus revenue-per-customer potential,
- how to combine software with payments, AI, automation, or services where appropriate,
- how to build a strong go-to-market model for a niche market,
- how to improve retention, stickiness, and expansion,
- and how to build a vertical SaaS company that becomes deeply embedded and hard to displace.

The goal is to avoid a common founder mistake:

**choosing a vertical because it sounds underserved, while underestimating workflow complexity, buyer nuance, compliance, implementation burden, distribution realities, and the difference between a small niche and a real category opportunity.**

These prompts are built around real vertical SaaS operating themes such as:
- market and category selection,
- workflow depth,
- niche economics,
- vertical GTM design,
- embedded payments or fintech where relevant,
- AI and labor-replacement opportunities,
- onboarding and implementation,
- retention and switching costs,
- expansion paths,
- and vertical-specific defensibility.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- choosing a vertical that sounds niche-attractive but is economically weak
- overestimating how much product breadth is needed before product-market fit exists
- underestimating implementation and change-management burden
- adding payments, fintech, or AI because the narrative is attractive rather than because the fit is real
- forcing horizontal-SaaS GTM logic onto a vertical market with different density and trust dynamics
- building a product that is useful but not embedded deeply enough to retain customers
- ignoring the difference between a small niche and a low-LTV dead end
- claiming vertical defensibility without true workflow embedding or switching costs
- building a vertical SaaS business around category stories instead of category economics
- confusing startup activity with a real, repeatable vertical SaaS operating system

The goal is to force clarity on how a founder should choose the right industry, design around real workflows, build revenue and retention depth, and create a vertical SaaS business that can truly dominate a niche instead of just serving it superficially.
