# Solopreneur Command Center, KPI Thresholds, Decision Rules, and Scale or Kill Criteria Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building a **solopreneur decision-control operating system** that turns strategy, finance, sales, marketing, product, and operations into clear actions.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **solopreneur founder** who wants to know:
- what metrics actually matter,
- what thresholds should trigger action,
- when to scale,
- when to pause,
- when to cut,
- when to raise prices,
- when to stop investing in a channel,
- when to retire an offer,
- when to reduce customer complexity,
- and how to make hard decisions before drift, hope, and sunk-cost thinking destroy profitability.

The goal is to avoid a common solopreneur mistake:

**having lots of data, instincts, and ideas, but no disciplined decision rules for protecting profit, preserving capital, and scaling only what is actually working.**

This pack is designed as the final control layer that sits on top of the other systems you have already built:
- strategy
- finance
- marketing
- sales
- product
- operations
- customer management
- legal/compliance
- execution

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- tracking metrics without acting on them
- keeping weak offers, channels, or segments alive too long
- failing to raise prices or cut costs soon enough
- scaling before proof exists
- confusing activity spikes with durable traction
- tolerating complexity until it quietly destroys margins
- delaying hard decisions until cash or energy is already damaged
- using monthly reviews for reflection instead of action
- building a dashboard that feels sophisticated but protects nothing
- having no formal decision rules for protecting profit, preserving capital, and scaling intelligently

The goal is to force clarity on how a solopreneur should govern the business through metrics, thresholds, triggers, and hard choices before drift, noise, and delayed action compromise financial success.
