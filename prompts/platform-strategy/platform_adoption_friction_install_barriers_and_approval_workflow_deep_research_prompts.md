# Platform Adoption Friction, Install Barriers, and Approval Workflow Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on **what blocks adoption after interest exists** across platform ecosystems.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **third-party developer** trying to understand:
- what makes a user hesitate,
- what prevents installation,
- what slows approval,
- what creates setup drag,
- what causes abandonment between evaluation and activation,
- what roles or permissions introduce friction,
- what trust and governance barriers must be cleared,
- and how these barriers differ across platforms.

The goal is to avoid a common platform-business mistake:

**mistaking desire for adoptability, or product-market interest for installable, approvable, retained software.**

This pack covers the current platform set:
- Shopify
- HubSpot
- Atlassian
- Monday.com
- Webflow
- Canva
- Twitch
- Kick
- Google Workspace Marketplace

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- assuming interest automatically becomes installation
- ignoring the difference between install permission and budget authority
- underestimating security, scope, or governance concerns
- building onboarding as if all users are self-serve and low-risk
- pricing as if adoption friction has no effect on conversion
- confusing configuration completion with real activation
- underestimating how long enterprise or admin-controlled approval cycles take
- ignoring post-install friction that causes silent churn before value is reached

The goal is to force clarity on what actually blocks adoption after desire exists, and how a product must be designed to survive that friction.
