# Platform Dependency and Platform-Risk Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on platform dependency, host-platform exposure, and ecosystem risk conducted entirely through public information and LLM-assisted analysis, without interviews or surveys.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — How Exposed Is This Product Category to Platform Risk?

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use interviews, surveys, invented examples, or unsupported speculation. Use current sources where recency matters. Distinguish clearly between:
1. verified facts,
2. reasoned inference,
3. uncertain or contested conclusions.

For every major claim, cite sources. Prioritize primary sources, official platform documentation, API docs, policy pages, developer terms, marketplace listings, changelogs, public case studies, review ecosystems, credible industry research, comparison content, and high-quality operator commentary when directly relevant.

Your task is not merely to summarize. Your task is to identify commercially meaningful platform dependencies, host-platform incentives, control points, policy risks, technical chokepoints, ecosystem dynamics, and strategic implications.

For each section of the answer, include:
- what is known,
- what appears to be changing,
- what the market may be underestimating,
- what this implies for a founder managing platform dependency and strategic exposure.

Where evidence is weak, say so explicitly.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “How exposed is the category of [INSERT PRODUCT OR CATEGORY] to platform risk?”

Analyze risk vectors such as:
- host-platform copy risk,
- API dependency,
- policy and terms-of-service changes,
- review and listing control,
- ranking and discovery dependence,
- billing dependence,
- data-access dependence,
- integration permission changes,
- pricing rule changes,
- ecosystem incentive misalignment,
- deprecation risk,
- compliance and security policy shifts.

Then produce:
- a category-specific platform-risk analysis,
- the strongest sources of platform exposure,
- the weakest or most overstated platform-risk fears,
- a framework for distinguishing manageable dependency from strategic fragility,
- a final recommendation for whether a new entrant should avoid, embrace, or cautiously enter the category.

Before finalizing the answer, perform an explicit internal adversarial review of your own conclusions. Identify at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, biased, overly optimistic, or too dependent on limited public evidence. Then revise the answer accordingly.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. What the Evidence Shows
3. The Real Sources of Platform Risk in This Category

5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
```
