# Platform Customers and Purchase Decision-Maker Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on **who the real customers are** and **who actually makes, influences, approves, installs, pays for, or blocks purchase decisions** across platform ecosystems.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **third-party developer** trying to understand:
- who uses the product,
- who wants the product,
- who can authorize installation,
- who controls budget,
- who influences selection,
- who owns procurement, security, or admin approval,
- who becomes the internal champion,
- and how those roles differ by platform.

The goal is to avoid a common platform-business mistake:

**confusing the user with the buyer, the buyer with the approver, or the approver with the economic decision maker.**

This pack covers the current platform set:
- Shopify
- HubSpot
- Atlassian
- Monday.com
- Webflow
- Canva
- Twitch
- Kick
- Google Workspace Marketplace

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

```text

- internal champion,
- evaluator,

- admin approver,

- and post-purchase operator.

You must explicitly distinguish among:
- who feels the pain,
- who searches for solutions,
- who is allowed to install,
- who can approve or reject,
- who controls spending,
- who carries risk for a bad decision,
- who renews or cancels.

Now conduct deep research across the following platforms:
- Shopify
- HubSpot
- Atlassian
- Monday.com
- Webflow
- Canva
- Twitch
- Kick
- Google Workspace Marketplace

Your goal is to map who the likely customers and purchase decision makers are for each platform from the perspective of a third-party developer.

For each platform, analyze:
- who is most likely to directly use the app,
- who is most likely to seek out the app,
- who is technically able to install or approve the app,
- who is most likely to control budget,
- who is most likely to care about security, data access, or governance,

Use this exact output structure:

5. Critical Platform Differences
```

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- building for the wrong persona
- selling to a user who cannot install
- optimizing marketing for a champion who cannot pay
- underestimating the role of admins, approvers, or procurement
- confusing creator enthusiasm with budget control
- pricing as if self-serve buyers and enterprise approvers behave the same way
- underestimating the gap between adoption friction and purchase intent

The goal is to force clarity on who actually decides, who actually pays, and who actually keeps the tool alive after adoption.
