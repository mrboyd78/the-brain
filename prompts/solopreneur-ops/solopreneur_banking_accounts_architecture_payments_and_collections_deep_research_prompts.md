# Solopreneur Banking, Accounts Architecture, Payments, and Collections Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building a **solopreneur money-movement system** that keeps banking, accounts architecture, payments, invoice collection, and cash handling clean, controlled, and resilient.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **solopreneur founder** who wants to understand:
- how to structure bank accounts,
- how money should move between accounts,
- how to separate operating cash from tax cash and reserves,
- how to handle client payments or subscriptions,
- how to manage invoices, failed payments, refunds, and disputes,
- how to reduce collection drag,
- how to avoid cash confusion caused by poor account architecture,
- and how to build payment and collections workflows that stay manageable without a finance team.

The goal is to avoid a common solopreneur mistake:

**having revenue coming in but no clean money system, no collection discipline, and no reliable way to know what cash is truly available.**

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- treating all cash as one undifferentiated pool
- having no clean distinction between tax money, reserve money, and operating money
- routing revenue through too many channels to understand what is actually happening
- letting invoices age because collections discipline was never formalized
- losing recurring revenue to failed payments and weak dunning
- allowing autopay and vendor sprawl to quietly erode control
- overcomplicating the account structure until it becomes unmaintainable
- relying too heavily on one bank or one processor without resilience planning
- confusing bank balance with truly available operating cash
- building a money system that is either too loose to protect the business or too bloated to maintain consistently

The goal is to force clarity on how money should enter, move through, and leave a solopreneur business without becoming confusing, fragile, or dangerous.
