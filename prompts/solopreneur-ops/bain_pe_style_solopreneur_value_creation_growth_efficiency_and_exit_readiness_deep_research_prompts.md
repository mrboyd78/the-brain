# Bain / PE-Style Solopreneur Value Creation, Growth Efficiency, and Exit Readiness Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on running a one-person business with **private-equity-style value creation discipline**.

They are tailored for the perspective of a **solopreneur founder** who wants to think about the business the way a strong PE operating team would think about a portfolio company:
- value creation by design,
- growth efficiency rather than growth theater,
- margin expansion with execution discipline,
- diligence before commitment,
- operating improvement before optimism,
- strategic pruning before sprawl,
- and exit readiness as a lens for building a higher-quality asset.

This is not a pack for casual small-business advice.

This is a pack for asking:
- where enterprise value is actually being created,
- what operational improvements matter most,
- whether growth is efficient enough,
- how to improve margins without degrading the business,
- whether the business could withstand diligence,
- and how to build a solo business that is not just income-producing, but increasingly valuable and more transferable.

These prompts are intentionally designed to emulate the rigor, structure, and decision quality associated with Bain-style private-equity value creation thinking, especially themes Bain regularly emphasizes in its private equity work:
- value creation plans,
- top-line growth,
- cost improvement and margin expansion,
- integrated diligence,
- operational execution,
- portfolio focus,
- technology and AI-enabled productivity,
- and exit readiness.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- treating a solo business as current-income only instead of a value-creation asset
- growing revenue without improving growth efficiency
- assuming margin expansion will happen without disciplined execution
- committing time or capital without sufficient diligence
- confusing operational activity with real operating improvement
- adding technology and AI without ROI discipline
- letting offers, channels, or products sprawl until complexity destroys value
- ignoring transferability and exit readiness because the business is small
- borrowing PE ideas without adapting them to the realities of one-person capacity
- building a business that looks active but would fail serious diligence

The goal is to force clarity on how a solopreneur should increase asset quality, improve economics, strengthen operating discipline, and build a more valuable business with the seriousness of a PE operating team rather than the looseness of generic internet business advice.
