# Solopreneur Entity Structure, Compliance, Insurance, and Legal-Risk Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building a **solopreneur legal and compliance operating system** that keeps a solo business appropriately structured, properly registered, compliance-aware, risk-conscious, and not accidentally exposed.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **solopreneur founder** who wants to understand:
- which entity structure fits the business,
- how legal structure affects taxes, liability, and administration,
- what registrations, IDs, and filings are actually required,
- what compliance obligations must be tracked,
- what insurance is worth carrying,
- what contracts and policies reduce avoidable risk,
- when local or state rules matter,
- and when professional legal or tax support becomes necessary.

The goal is to avoid a common solopreneur mistake:

**running a real business with real liability and tax exposure while relying on vague assumptions about entity choice, compliance, and legal protection.**

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- choosing an entity based on hearsay rather than real tradeoffs
- forming an entity without understanding its maintenance obligations
- getting the EIN and formation sequence wrong
- missing state and local compliance because federal registration felt like enough
- assuming BOI or other reporting obligations based on outdated information
- operating without sufficient insurance for the actual risk profile
- relying on handshake agreements or weak templates for important client and contractor work
- blurring personal and business operations until liability protection is weakened
- waiting too long to get legal or tax help when the situation has already become expensive
- building a legal-risk system that is either too weak to protect the business or too bloated to maintain consistently

The goal is to force clarity on how a solopreneur should structure, register, protect, document, and maintain a business before avoidable legal and compliance problems start compounding.
