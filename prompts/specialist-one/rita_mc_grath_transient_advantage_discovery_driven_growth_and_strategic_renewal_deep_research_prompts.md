# Rita McGrath Transient Advantage, Discovery-Driven Growth, and Strategic Renewal Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on the **strategy insights, frameworks, and teachings of Rita McGrath**.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, operator, strategist, innovation leader, SaaS builder, or investor** who wants to understand:
- what Rita McGrath actually teaches,
- why she argues sustainable advantage is often no longer the right default goal,
- how transient advantage works,
- how discovery-driven growth reduces uncertainty,
- how strategic renewal and resource reallocation should operate,
- how firms compete in fast-changing environments,
- and where her frameworks are strongest or limited.

The goal is to avoid a common mistake:

**treating McGrath as simply anti-moat or anti-strategy, while missing her deeper logic about opportunity life cycles, strategic agility, experimentation, and renewal under uncertainty.**

These prompts are built around her primary themes, especially:
- transient competitive advantage,
- discovery-driven growth,
- strategic growth and change,
- resource reallocation,
- and renewal in volatile environments.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- treating all advantages as durable by default
- defending old strengths after they have begun to erode
- confusing agility with improvisation
- pursuing growth without disciplined assumption testing
- underinvesting in renewal and reallocation
- importing enterprise strategy into startups without adaptation
- using McGrath’s language without understanding its operational meaning
- treating her work as anti-moat rather than anti-static thinking
- ignoring the portfolio and timing implications of transient advantage
- building strategy around stability assumptions that no longer hold

The goal is to force clarity on what Rita McGrath actually teaches, how her frameworks fit together, where they are strongest, and how to apply them without flattening them into generic agility advice.
