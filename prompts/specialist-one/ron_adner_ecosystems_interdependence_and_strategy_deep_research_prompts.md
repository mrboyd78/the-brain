# Ron Adner Ecosystems, Interdependence, and Strategy Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on the **strategy insights, frameworks, and teachings of Ron Adner**.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, operator, strategist, product leader, SaaS builder, or investor** who wants to understand:
- what Ron Adner actually teaches,
- how ecosystem strategy differs from firm-centric strategy,
- what interdependence means strategically,
- how value creation and value capture change in ecosystems,
- how to think about ecosystem disruption, offense, and defense,
- how his ideas apply to platforms, software, and market transitions,
- and where his frameworks are strongest or context-bound.

The goal is to avoid a common mistake:

**talking about ecosystems loosely, while missing Adner’s specific analytical tools for alignment risk, co-innovation risk, ecosystem positioning, and competing in an interdependent world.**

These prompts are built around Adner’s real body of work, especially:
- *The Wide Lens*,
- *Winning the Right Game*,
- ecosystem disruption,
- interdependence,
- value architecture,
- and ecosystem strategy.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — Build a Complete Map of Ron Adner’s Ecosystem Strategy Philosophy

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use invented examples, unsupported anecdotes, or secondhand summaries unless clearly labeled as secondary commentary. Use current official and high-authority sources.

Distinguish clearly between verified facts, reasoned inference, and uncertainty.

Prioritize sources in this order:
1. Ron Adner’s official site and books,
2. official excerpts, talks, and institutional profiles,
3. high-quality commentary only as secondary support.

Your task is not merely to summarize Ron Adner. Your task is to synthesize the full architecture of his strategy philosophy.

The analysis must explicitly cover:
- ecosystems,
- interdependence,
- ecosystem disruption,
- value creation,
- value capture,
- strategy under changing competitive boundaries,
- offense and defense,
- and the broader logic of how firms compete when success depends on others.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “What is the complete underlying strategy philosophy Ron Adner teaches across his books, official materials, and ecosystem frameworks?”

Then produce:
- a complete philosophy map,
- the core recurring frameworks,
- the strategic logic connecting them,
- the major tradeoffs and caveats,
- and a final recommendation for how to interpret Adner accurately.

Before finalizing, perform an explicit internal adversarial review identifying at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, or overly reductive, then revise.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. Adner’s Core Strategy Philosophy
3. Main Frameworks and Mental Models
4. Strategic Tradeoffs and Caveats
5. Common Misreadings
6. Strategic Implications for Founders and Strategists
7. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
8. Final Recommendations
9. Source List
```

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- using “ecosystem” as a vague buzzword
- ignoring dependencies outside the firm boundary
- underestimating alignment and co-innovation risk
- treating rivalry as only firm-versus-firm
- missing partner-to-rival shifts
- overapplying platform logic where it does not fit
- ignoring delivery dependencies in innovation plans
- using ecosystem language without analytical precision
- confusing visibility with ecosystem position
- building strategy as if success were under unilateral control

The goal is to force clarity on what Ron Adner actually teaches, how his ecosystem frameworks fit together, where they are strongest, and how to apply them without looseness or jargon drift.
