# W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne Blue Ocean, Market Creation, and Nondisruptive Innovation Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on the **strategy insights, frameworks, and teachings of W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne**.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, strategist, SaaS builder, operator, innovator, or investor** who wants to understand:
- what Kim and Mauborgne actually teach,
- how blue ocean strategy works,
- how value innovation differs from competition-led strategy,
- how market-creating moves are identified,
- how blue ocean shifts are executed,
- how nondisruptive creation changes innovation logic,
- and where their frameworks are strongest or limited.

The goal is to avoid a common mistake:

**reducing Blue Ocean Strategy to “differentiate more” or “find an underserved niche,” while missing the deeper logic of value innovation, market-creating moves, noncustomer insight, and the newer nondisruptive creation framework.**

These prompts are built around their primary themes, especially:
- Blue Ocean Strategy,
- Blue Ocean Shift,
- value innovation,
- market creation,
- noncustomers,
- blue ocean leadership where relevant,
- and Beyond Disruption / nondisruptive creation.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

```text

Prioritize sources in this order:
1. INSEAD and Blue Ocean Strategy Institute materials,
2. official materials tied to Blue Ocean Strategy, Blue Ocean Shift, and Beyond Disruption,
3. direct talks, interviews, and official articles,
4. high-quality commentary only as secondary support.

Your task is not merely to summarize Kim and Mauborgne. Your task is to synthesize the full architecture of their strategy philosophy.

The analysis must explicitly cover:
- blue oceans,
- value innovation,
- strategic moves,
- noncustomers,
- blue ocean shifts,
- nondisruptive creation,
- and how these frameworks fit together.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “What is the complete underlying strategic philosophy W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne teach across their major frameworks and official materials?”

Then produce:

- the major tradeoffs and caveats,

Before finalizing, perform an explicit internal adversarial review identifying at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, or overly reductive, then revise.
```

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- confusing blue oceans with differentiation slogans
- ignoring value innovation’s cost-and-buyer-value logic
- turning blue-ocean tools into workshop rituals without strategy
- overlooking noncustomers as a source of new demand
- overextending blue-ocean language into every niche choice
- ignoring the difference between disruptive and nondisruptive creation
- forcing their frameworks onto SaaS without adaptation
- using their language without understanding the doctrine behind it
- reducing their work to one book while ignoring later developments
- missing the distinction between competing better and creating new demand

The goal is to force clarity on what Kim and Mauborgne actually teach, how their frameworks fit together, where they are strongest, and how to apply them without flattening them into generic innovation talk.
