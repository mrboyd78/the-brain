# Thales Teixeira Disruption, Decoupling, and Demand-Side Strategy Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on the **strategy insights, frameworks, and teachings of Thales Teixeira**.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, solopreneur, SaaS builder, operator, strategist, product business, or investor** who wants to understand:
- what Thales Teixeira actually teaches,
- how his decoupling framework works,
- why he argues that customer behavior matters more than technology in many disruption stories,
- how to apply his ideas to startup growth, product strategy, GTM, and competitive response,
- how his work on attention economics connects to his disruption thinking,
- where his frameworks are strongest,
- and where his ideas may be context-bound, incomplete, or in tension with other strategy doctrines.

The goal is to avoid a common mistake:

**reducing Thales Teixeira to a generic “disruption theorist,” while missing the specific demand-side logic, customer value chain analysis, attention dynamics, and response strategies that run through his body of work.**

These prompts are built around Teixeira’s actual published and official work, including themes reflected across:
- his official Decoupling site,
- his book *Unlocking the Customer Value Chain*,
- his Harvard Business School interviews and research summaries,
- his work on attention economics,
- his startup-growth case insights,
- and his executive workshops on disruption and response strategy.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- reducing Teixeira’s work to generic disruption language
- overfocusing on technology while missing his demand-side logic
- using the word “decoupling” without understanding the customer value chain
- missing the connection between attention economics and later disruption thinking
- assuming his consumer examples transfer automatically to all markets without adaptation
- treating his frameworks as slogans rather than analytical tools
- missing his emphasis on value leakage and customer-activity capture
- oversimplifying how incumbents should respond to disruption
- ignoring the evolution from research into applied strategy
- borrowing his ideas selectively in ways that create contradiction

The goal is to force clarity on what Thales Teixeira actually teaches, how his frameworks fit together, where they are strongest, and how a founder or strategist should apply them without caricature.
