# Tony Ulwick Jobs to Be Done and Outcome-Driven Innovation Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on the **strategy insights, frameworks, and teachings of Tony Ulwick**.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **founder, product leader, strategist, SaaS builder, operator, or innovator** who wants to understand:
- what Tony Ulwick actually teaches,
- how his version of Jobs to Be Done differs from other JTBD schools,
- how Outcome-Driven Innovation works,
- how desired outcomes should be used to prioritize innovation,
- how markets should be defined through jobs rather than products,
- how growth options should be chosen from a jobs lens,
- and where Ulwick’s frameworks are strongest or limited.

The goal is to avoid a common mistake:

**using Jobs to Be Done as a loose customer-interview mindset while missing Ulwick’s much more structured, measurable, and prioritization-oriented innovation system.**

These prompts are built around Ulwick’s primary themes, especially:
- Jobs to Be Done,
- Outcome-Driven Innovation,
- desired outcomes,
- market definition through jobs,
- innovation predictability,
- and growth strategy from the job lens.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — Build a Complete Map of Tony Ulwick’s Innovation Philosophy

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use invented examples, unsupported anecdotes, or secondhand summaries unless clearly labeled as secondary commentary. Use current official and high-authority sources.

Distinguish clearly between verified facts, reasoned inference, and uncertainty.

Prioritize sources in this order:
1. Strategyn official materials and Tony Ulwick’s official pages,
2. Ulwick’s books and official summaries,
3. direct webinars, talks, and official articles,
4. high-quality commentary only as secondary support.

Your task is not merely to summarize Tony Ulwick. Your task is to synthesize the full architecture of his innovation philosophy.

The analysis must explicitly cover:
- Jobs to Be Done,
- Outcome-Driven Innovation,
- desired outcomes,
- innovation predictability,
- market definition,
- growth strategy,
- and how his frameworks fit together.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “What is the complete underlying innovation philosophy Tony Ulwick teaches across his official materials and frameworks?”

Then produce:
- a complete philosophy map,
- the core recurring frameworks,
- the strategic logic connecting them,
- the major tradeoffs and caveats,
- and a final recommendation for how to interpret Ulwick accurately.

Before finalizing, perform an explicit internal adversarial review identifying at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, or overly reductive, then revise.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. Ulwick’s Core Innovation Philosophy
3. Main Frameworks and Mental Models
4. Strategic Tradeoffs and Caveats
5. Common Misreadings
6. Strategic Implications for Founders and Product Leaders
```

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- treating JTBD as a vague interview method only
- confusing jobs with personas, features, or categories
- skipping measurable outcomes in innovation prioritization
- brainstorming solutions before identifying unmet outcomes
- defining markets by products instead of jobs
- forcing enterprise consulting doctrine onto startups without adaptation
- using Ulwick’s language without understanding ODI’s structure
- overclaiming predictability without doing the measurement work
- flattening his framework into generic customer-centricity
- missing how his method changes growth strategy and segmentation

The goal is to force clarity on what Tony Ulwick actually teaches, how his frameworks fit together, where they are strongest, and how to apply them without reducing them to generic JTBD talk.
