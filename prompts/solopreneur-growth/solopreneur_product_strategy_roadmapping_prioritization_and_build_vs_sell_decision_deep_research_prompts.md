# Solopreneur Product Strategy, Roadmapping, Prioritization, and Build-vs-Sell Decision Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on building a **solopreneur product-decision operating system** that keeps product strategy, prioritization, roadmapping, scope control, and build-versus-sell decisions disciplined enough to create real value without trapping the founder in endless feature work.

They are specifically designed for the perspective of a **solopreneur founder** who wants to understand:
- what to build first,
- what not to build,
- how to prioritize without reacting to every idea,
- how to roadmap without fantasy planning,
- how to balance customer requests against product strategy,
- how to evaluate strategic fit, revenue impact, and execution cost,
- how to decide between building, buying, partnering, or ignoring,
- and how to create product progress that actually improves the business.

The goal is to avoid a common solopreneur mistake:

**mistaking motion for product strategy, roadmapping too far into uncertainty, building too much custom functionality, and spending scarce founder time on work that does not materially improve demand, delivery, retention, or defensibility.**

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## What This Pack Is Supposed to Prevent

This pack is designed to prevent the following strategic mistakes:
- building around weak or low-value problems
- prioritizing by emotion, novelty, or request volume instead of leverage
- roadmapping with false precision
- shipping too many features that create more maintenance than value
- letting loud customer requests hijack the roadmap
- building internally what should have been bought, partnered, or ignored
- confusing activity with validation
- measuring product motion instead of business impact
- overbuilding because founder attachment overrides strategic discipline
- building a product-decision system that is either too weak to protect scarce capacity or too bloated to maintain consistently

The goal is to force clarity on how a solopreneur should choose what to build, what to delay, what to cut, and what to never touch before product work turns into expensive drift.
