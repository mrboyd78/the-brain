# Product-Market Fit Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on product-market fit conducted entirely through public information and LLM-assisted analysis, without interviews or surveys.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — What Does Real Product-Market Fit Actually Look Like in This Category?

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use interviews, surveys, invented examples, or unsupported speculation. Use current sources where recency matters. Distinguish clearly between:
1. verified facts,
2. reasoned inference,
3. uncertain or contested conclusions.

For every major claim, cite sources. Prioritize primary sources, official documentation, pricing pages, earnings materials, product reviews, marketplace listings, security reports, public case studies, credible industry research, and high-quality operator commentary when directly relevant.

Your task is not merely to summarize. Your task is to identify commercially meaningful patterns, market tensions, buyer incentives, implementation burdens, demand signals, and strategic implications.

For each section of the answer, include:
- what is known,
- what appears to be changing,
- what the market may be underestimating,
- what this implies for a founder trying to reach product-market fit.

Where evidence is weak, say so explicitly.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “What does real product-market fit actually look like in the category of [INSERT CATEGORY OR PRODUCT TYPE]?”

Your analysis must include:
- how demand manifests in this category,
- which signals indicate strong pull rather than shallow interest,
- how buyers in this category evaluate value,
- what retention or expansion indicators matter most,
- how product-market fit differs for SMB, mid-market, and enterprise if relevant,
- how to distinguish true fit from temporary novelty, discount-fueled growth, or AI hype.

Then produce:
- a definition of product-market fit specific to this category,
- a list of observable public indicators that suggest fit,
- a list of misleading indicators that can create false confidence,
- a framework for determining whether a product in this category has likely reached real fit.

Before finalizing the answer, perform an explicit internal adversarial review of your own conclusions. Identify at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, biased, overly optimistic, or too dependent on limited public evidence. Then revise the answer accordingly.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. What the Evidence Shows
3. What Real Product-Market Fit Looks Like in This Category
4. False Positives and Misleading Signals
5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
6. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
7. Final Recommendations
8. Source List
```
