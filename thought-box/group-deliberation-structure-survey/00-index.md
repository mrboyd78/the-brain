## Major Contradictions Flagged Across Literature

| Claim | High-Profile Study | Meta-Analytic Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| High cohesion → poor decisions (groupthink) | Janis (1972, 1982) | **Contradicted**: Mullen & Copper (1994) meta shows *r* = +.25 cohesion–performance |
| 6-person jury = functional equivalent of 12 | *Williams v. Florida* (SCOTUS, 1970) | **Contradicted**: Saks & Marti (1997) meta shows systematic quality deficit in 6-person panels |
| Group deliberation improves on individual judgment | Common assumption | **Context-dependent**: Only true with structured process; otherwise, conformity cascades and process losses dominate |
| Demographic diversity reliably improves decisions | Policy advocacy literature | **Inconsistent**: Effect is null-to-negative without information elaboration structures; Stasser & Titus (1985) shows why |
| Majority rule leads to good decisions | Practical assumption | **Nuanced**: Majority rule is robust but silences minority voice and reduces hung-verdict rate (signal of genuine uncertainty) |
