# Deliverable 5: Unified Evidence Grading Scale (UEGS)

> **Goal:** Propose a 5–7 band grading scale that reconciles the legal, medical, climate, and intelligence frameworks using evidence from the cross-walk and elicited numerical anchors.

---

## 5.1 Design Philosophy: The 6-Band "Actionability" Scale

The **Unified Evidence Grading Scale (UEGS)** is designed to address the major failure modes identified in the survey—specifically the 11-point "Likely" gap between climate and intelligence frameworks and the lack of intermediate bands in legal standards. 

The UEGS adopts a **6-band structure**. Six bands provide enough granularity to distinguish between "fairly certain" and "certain" (crucial for legal/medical contexts) while maintaining a sub-preponderance "Emerging" band for early-stage scientific or intelligence findings.

---

## 5.2 The Unified Scale (UEGS) Master Table

This table serves as the centerpiece of the synthesis, mapping the UEGS to the five survey frameworks.

| UEGS Band | Numerical Anchor | Summary Definition | Legal Analog | GRADE | IPCC / IC |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Certainty** | **99% – 100%** | Definitively established; no credible doubt remains | — | High (⊕⊕⊕⊕) | Virtually Certain |
| **High Confidence** | **90% – 99%** | Firm belief with only remote chance of error | **Beyond Reasonable Doubt** | High / Moderate | Very Likely |
| **Substantial Evidence** | **75% – 90%** | Strongly supported; unlikely to be changed by new data | **Clear & Convincing** | Moderate (⊕⊕⊕○) | Likely (Upper) |
| **Preponderance** | **51% – 75%** | More likely than not; affirmative support | **Preponderance** | Moderate / Low | Likely (Lower) |
| **Emerging Support** | **33% – 51%** | Suggestive evidence but lacks decisive weight | — | Low (⊕⊕○○) | More Liked Than Not |
| **Uncertain / Remote** | **< 33%** | Speculative or contradicted by current data | — | Very Low (⊕○○○) | Unlikely / Remote |

## 5.3 Derivation Rationale

### 5.3.1 The "High Confidence" Boundary (90%)
The 90% threshold for **High Confidence** is derived from the convergence of legal **Beyond a Reasonable Doubt** (juror elicitation median: 90%) and the IC/IPCC **Very Likely** standards. Setting this anchor at 90% creates a clear "Gold Standard" for actionable decisions where the cost of a false positive is high (e.g., criminal conviction, massive infrastructure investment).

### 5.3.2 The "Substantial" vs. "Preponderance" Split (75%)
A major divergence found in the research was the wide range of the word "Likely." The UEGS splits this into two bands:
- **Substantial Evidence (75%–90%)**: Mapped to the legal **Clear and Convincing** standard (*Addington v. Texas*) and the upper tier of GRADE's **Moderate** certainty. This is the "clear winner" band.
- **Preponderance (51%–75%)**: Mapped to the base civil burden of proof. This allows the scale to maintain the ICD 203 "Likely" definition (≥55%) without conflating it with the more robust standards required for high-stakes medical or legal conclusions.

### 5.3.3 The "Emerging" Band (33%–51%)
Unlike legal frameworks which treat anything <50.1% as a failure, the UEGS includes an **Emerging Support** band. This is derivable from the IPCC "More likely than not" and GRADE "Low" categories. It formally recognizes evidence that is not yet sufficient for positive assertion but carries significantly more weight than "Remote" speculation.

---

## 5.4 Unified Downgrading Protocol

To ensure consistency in applying these grades, the UEGS adopts a "Universal Foundation" based on the patterns identified in Deliverable 3.

**A UEGS grade MUST be downgraded by one band if any of the following are met:**
1. **Source Singularity**: Conclusion rests on a single uncorroborated line of evidence (ICD 206 / GRADE Inconsistency).
2. **Relevancy Gap**: Evidence is indirect or relies on surrogate endpoints (GRADE Indirectness / Legal Relevance).
3. **Internal Contradiction**: Multiple high-quality sources provide conflicting results (IPCC Agreement / Science replication failure).
4. **Transparency Failure**: Hidden methodology or high risk of reporting bias (GRADE Publication Bias / IC D&D analysis).
