## 6.1 Legal Standards of Proof

- **In re Winship**, 397 U.S. 358 (1970).  
  *Context:* Established "beyond a reasonable doubt" as a constitutional requirement for juvenile delinquency proceedings, extending the standard to all criminal contexts.
- **Addington v. Texas**, 441 U.S. 418 (1979).  
  *Context:* Established the "clear and convincing evidence" standard for civil commitment proceedings, balancing individual liberty against state interests.
- **Victor v. Nebraska**, 511 U.S. 1 (1994).  
  *Context:* Supreme Court review of jury instructions defining "reasonable doubt" and "moral certainty."
- **Santosky v. Kramer**, 455 U.S. 745 (1982).  
  *Context:* Established "clear and convincing evidence" as the minimum standard for termination of parental rights.
- **Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals**, 509 U.S. 579 (1993). DOI: [10.1056/NEJM199310283291823](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJM199310283291823)  
  *Context:* Defined the "gatekeeper" role of federal judges in assessing scientific evidence reliability.
