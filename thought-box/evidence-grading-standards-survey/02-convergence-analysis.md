# Deliverable 2: Convergence Analysis

**Research question:** Which bands correspond across frameworks within ±10 probability points? Where do they diverge by more?

---

## 2.1 Methodology

Convergence is assessed by comparing the *operative probability threshold* of each band across frameworks. Where a framework uses a range (e.g., IPCC Likely = 66–100%), the midpoint or lower bound is used as the primary comparator, as the lower bound is the decision-relevant threshold (the point at which a claim is first "promoted" to that probability tier).

**Convergence criterion:**
- **Strong convergence:** Two or more frameworks place equivalent bands within ±10 probability points of each other
- **Moderate convergence:** Within ±15 probability points
- **Divergence:** More than ±15 probability points apart, or no equivalent band exists

## 2.3 Zones of Strong Convergence

### Convergence Zone 1: "Near-Certainty" (p = 90–99%)

All five frameworks recognize this as a distinct, meaningful high-confidence zone:

| Framework | Term | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Beyond a reasonable doubt (BRD) | 90–95% (empirical) |
| GRADE | High certainty | ~85–99% (implied) |
| IPCC | Very likely | 90–100% |
| ICD 203 | Very likely / Very probably | 80–95% |
| Scientific consensus | Strong consensus | 90–99% |

**Convergence assessment: Strong.** All five frameworks place a distinct meaningful band in the 90–99% range. The specific labels vary but the functional meaning is equivalent: decision-makers across all fields treat this zone as the threshold for confident action without significant epistemic caveats.

**Residual divergence within this zone:** GRADE's "High" range starts somewhat lower (~85%) than legal BRD (~90%), and IPCC "Very likely" covers 90–100% while ICD 203 "Very likely" covers 80–95% — a 10-point gap in the lower bound.

### Convergence Zone 2: "High Probability" (p = 66–80%)

| Framework | Term | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Clear and convincing evidence | 73–80% (empirical) |
| GRADE | Moderate certainty | 65–85% (implied) |
| IPCC | Likely | 66–100% (lower bound: 66%) |
| ICD 203 | Likely / Probably | 55–80% |
| Scientific consensus | Emerging/Near consensus | 65–80% |

**Convergence assessment: Moderate-to-strong.** There is broad agreement that 66–80% represents a distinct high-probability tier, though the upper bounds vary:
- IPCC "Likely" has a 34-point wide official range (66–100%)
- ICD 203 "Likely" has a 25-point range (55–80%)
- Legal "Clear and convincing" is empirically centered at 73–80%

The most important divergence here: **IPCC "Likely" begins at 66%, ICD 203 "Likely" begins at 55%** — a meaningful 11-point gap that is a frequent source of cross-domain confusion.

### Convergence Zone 3: "Uncertain / Near 50-50" (p = 40–60%)

| Framework | Term | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Irrelevant (below preponderance, case fails) | ≤50% |
| GRADE | Low / Very Low certainty | 45–65% (implied) |
| IPCC | More likely than not / About as likely as not | 33–66% |
| ICD 203 | Even chance | 45–55% |
| Scientific consensus | Actively contested | 40–65% |

**Convergence assessment: Moderate.** Most frameworks recognize this zone as distinct — the "coin-flip" territory where a claim cannot be confidently made. The exception is **legal reasoning**: below preponderance, the legal framework renders a binary verdict (defendant is not liable) rather than maintaining an explicit "uncertain" zone.

### Convergence Zone 4: "Low Probability" (p = 10–33%)

| Framework | Term | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | No equivalent standard | — |
| GRADE | Very Low certainty | <45% |
| IPCC | Unlikely | 0–33% |
| ICD 203 | Unlikely / Improbably | 20–45% |
| Scientific consensus | Minority view | 25–40% |

**Convergence assessment: Weak.** The major divergence is that legal frameworks have **no standard below preponderance** — once a claim fails the preponderance test, the framework simply renders judgment for the opposing party. IPCC and ICD 203 maintain multiple low-probability tiers (Unlikely, Very Unlikely, Exceptionally Unlikely / Remote) that have no legal parallels.

## 2.4 Major Divergences

### Divergence Point 1: The "Probably" / "Likely" Band Is Differently Placed (±15+ points)

The most important inter-framework divergence is the placement of the primary positive-probability assertion word:

| Framework | Primary "positive assertion" term | Lower bound |
|---|---|---|
| Legal BRD | Beyond reasonable doubt | ~90% |
| Legal C&C | Clear and convincing | ~73% |
| Legal Preponderance | More likely than not | ~51% |
| GRADE Moderate | Moderate certainty | ~65% |
| IPCC Likely | Likely | 66% |
| ICD 203 Likely | Likely / Probably | 55% |
| Kent Probable | Probably | 63% |
| Scientific Near-consensus | Near-consensus | ~70% |

**Gap between ICD 203 "Likely" (55%) and IPCC "Likely" (66%): 11 percentage points.** When an intelligence analyst says an event is "likely" (meaning ≥55% probable) and a climate scientist says a trend is "likely" (meaning ≥66% probable), they are using the same word with a substantive statistical difference. This is not a semantic quibble — an 11-point gap at this range represents a meaningful difference in actionability.

### Divergence Point 2: No Framework Below Legal Preponderance Has a Legal Equivalent

Legal standards are entirely **threshold-based**: below preponderance (50+ε%), the case fails. The other four frameworks maintain multiple sub-50% tiers with analytical meaning. This is a fundamental structural difference:

- **Legal:** Binary + thresholds. Either the standard is met, or it isn't. Below preponderance there is nothing — no "uncertain" verdict, no "probably not" verdict.
- **GRADE / IPCC / IC / Science:** Continuous spectrum. "Very Low" evidence or "Remote" probability are meaningful statements, not just failures.

### Divergence Point 3: Two-Dimensional vs. One-Dimensional Systems

**IPCC and IC frameworks use two distinct dimensions** (likelihood × confidence / probability × evidence quality) that are explicitly kept separate:
- IPCC: Likelihood scale (probability) × Confidence scale (evidence quality)
- ICD 203: Probability terms × Confidence levels (High / Moderate / Low)

**GRADE conflates these** into a single certainty grade: a "High" GRADE rating means both (a) the evidence is of high quality *and* (b) therefore we are confident the effect estimate is correct.

**Legal standards conflate them** even more completely: the standard of proof captures both dimensions in a single threshold determination. Courts do not separately report "we found the probability was 75% with High confidence" — they render a verdict.

**Scientific consensus statements** also tend toward single-dimension characterization (consensus = yes/no, or graded), without separating probability from evidence quality.

**Implication:** A 2×2 cross-framework comparison of evidence frameworks requires collapsing either the IPCC/IC two-dimensional output or the GRADE/legal one-dimensional output to make them comparable. There is no loss-free translation.

### Divergence Point 4: The "Certainty" Terminal Node

All frameworks except **legal** acknowledge that certainty exists as a distinct conceptual category:
- IPCC: "Virtually certain" (99–100%)
- ICD 203 / Kent: "Almost certain" / "Certain" (93–100%)
- GRADE: Implied in "High" but no separate "certain" tier
- Scientific consensus: "Established scientific fact" (but rarely labeled formally)

**Legal frameworks deliberately avoid certainty as a standard.** BRD is explicitly *not* certainty: *Jackson v. Virginia*, 443 U.S. 307 (1979) reaffirmed that "beyond a reasonable doubt does not mean beyond all doubt." This is the inverse of epistemic humility — it acknowledges that certainty is an impossible standard, so it sets the bar lower. No other framework makes this explicit philosophical move.

## 2.6 Summary of Convergence Findings

**Strong convergence** (within ±10 pp across all frameworks):
- The **≥90% zone** is universally recognized as "high confidence / near certain"
- The **≤10% zone** is universally recognized as "highly improbable"

**Moderate convergence** (within ±15 pp across most frameworks):
- The **66–80% zone** is broadly recognized as "probable / likely / clear"
- The **40–60% zone** is broadly recognized as "uncertain"

**Notable divergences** (>15 pp gap or structural incompatibility):
- **ICD 203 "Likely" starts at 55%; IPCC "Likely" starts at 66%** — 11-point gap in the primary positive-assertion term
- **Legal standards are binary and threshold-based; others are continuous** — structural incompatibility below the preponderance threshold
- **IPCC and IC use 2D frameworks; legal and GRADE use 1D** — dimensionality mismatch
- **No legal equivalent for low-probability tiers** (Unlikely, Very Unlikely, Remote)
- **GRADE's "High" starts at ~85%; legal BRD starts at ~90%** — 5-point gap that creates ambiguity when GRADE "High" evidence informs legal or policy thresholds
