# Competitive Positioning and Differentiation Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on competitive positioning and differentiation conducted entirely through public information and LLM-assisted analysis, without interviews or surveys.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — What Differentiation Actually Matters in This Category?

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use interviews, surveys, invented examples, or unsupported speculation. Use current sources where recency matters. Distinguish clearly between:
1. verified facts,
2. reasoned inference,
3. uncertain or contested conclusions.

For every major claim, cite sources. Prioritize primary sources, official product pages, pricing pages, documentation, marketplace listings, review ecosystems, public case studies, credible industry research, competitor comparisons, changelogs, and high-quality operator commentary when directly relevant.

Your task is not merely to summarize. Your task is to identify commercially meaningful positioning patterns, differentiation mechanisms, buyer decision criteria, category narratives, competitive blind spots, and strategic implications.

For each section of the answer, include:
- what is known,
- what appears to be changing,
- what the market may be underestimating,
- what this implies for a founder designing positioning and differentiation.

Where evidence is weak, say so explicitly.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “What forms of differentiation actually matter in the category of [INSERT PRODUCT OR CATEGORY]?”

Analyze dimensions such as:
- workflow depth,
- implementation speed,
- trust and governance,
- integration breadth,
- specialization,
- ease of use,
- performance,
- reliability,
- ROI clarity,
- support quality,
- ecosystem fit,
- pricing transparency,
- automation quality,
- compliance readiness,
- service-layer depth.

Then produce:
- a category-specific differentiation analysis,
- the strongest forms of differentiation,
- the weakest or most cosmetic differentiation claims,
- a framework for identifying real differentiation versus marketing noise,
- a final recommendation for how a new entrant should aim to stand apart.

Before finalizing the answer, perform an explicit internal adversarial review of your own conclusions. Identify at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, biased, overly optimistic, or too dependent on limited public evidence. Then revise the answer accordingly.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. What the Evidence Shows
3. What Differentiation Actually Matters in This Category
4. Weak or Cosmetic Differentiation Claims
5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
6. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
7. Final Recommendations
8. Source List
```
