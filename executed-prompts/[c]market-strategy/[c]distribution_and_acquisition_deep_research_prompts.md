# Distribution and Acquisition Deep Research Prompts

These prompts are designed for deep research on distribution and customer acquisition conducted entirely through public information and LLM-assisted analysis, without interviews or surveys.

Each prompt is copy-and-paste ready and includes:
- master operating instructions
- a rigor add-on
- a consistent output format

## Prompt 1 — Which Acquisition Channels Actually Fit This Product Category?

```text
Conduct deep research using only publicly available information. Do not use interviews, surveys, invented examples, or unsupported speculation. Use current sources where recency matters. Distinguish clearly between:
1. verified facts,
2. reasoned inference,
3. uncertain or contested conclusions.

For every major claim, cite sources. Prioritize primary sources, official platform documentation, marketplace listings, pricing pages, case studies, earnings materials, credible industry research, product reviews, public comparisons, public operator commentary, SEO visibility, partner directories, and high-quality ecosystem content when directly relevant.

Your task is not merely to summarize. Your task is to identify commercially meaningful distribution patterns, acquisition mechanisms, buyer discovery behavior, channel economics, competitive dynamics, and strategic implications.

For each section of the answer, include:
- what is known,
- what appears to be changing,
- what the market may be underestimating,
- what this implies for a founder designing distribution and acquisition.

Where evidence is weak, say so explicitly.

Now conduct deep research on the question: “Which acquisition channels actually fit the category of [INSERT PRODUCT OR CATEGORY]?”

Evaluate channels such as:
- marketplace discovery
- SEO
- content marketing
- partner and agency channels
- integrations as distribution
- communities
- newsletters
- social media
- outbound sales
- founder-led sales
- paid search
- paid social
- comparison-site visibility
- app-store rankings
- product-led referral loops
- affiliate programs

Analyze:
- how buyers in this category discover products,
- which channels appear most credible,
- which channels create high-intent demand,
- which channels are crowded or deteriorating,
- which channels are realistic for a solo founder or small team,
- how the acquisition channel must align with product complexity and sales motion.

Then produce:
- a category-specific acquisition-channel analysis,
- the strongest-fit channels,
- the weakest-fit channels,
- tradeoffs between speed, cost, durability, and trust,
- a final channel recommendation stack for a new entrant.

Before finalizing the answer, perform an explicit internal adversarial review of your own conclusions. Identify at least five ways your reasoning may be weak, incomplete, biased, overly optimistic, or too dependent on limited public evidence. Then revise the answer accordingly.

Use this exact output structure:
1. Executive Thesis
2. What the Evidence Shows
3. Which Acquisition Channels Best Fit This Category and Why
4. Weak or Mismatched Channels
5. Strategic Implications for a Founder
6. Risks, Counterarguments, and Uncertainty
7. Final Recommendations
8. Source List
```
