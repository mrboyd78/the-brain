# T12 — Assumption & Unknowns Register Standard

## Document Status
**System:** Market-Intelligence Operating System  
**Layer:** Companion Governance Layer  
**Designation:** T12  
**Document Type:** Standard  
**Status:** Canonical Draft  
**Purpose Class:** Uncertainty Governance / Assumption Control / Validation Readiness

---

## 1. Purpose

The **Assumption & Unknowns Register** is the authoritative uncertainty-governance artifact for the Market-Intelligence Operating System.

Its function is to identify, classify, govern, and track the assumptions, unresolved questions, missing knowledge, interpretive leaps, and confidence boundaries that remain after evidence collection and analysis.

The register exists to ensure that the system does not confuse:

- evidence with inference
- inference with conviction
- conviction with certainty
- missing knowledge with resolved knowledge

Its role is not to eliminate uncertainty. Its role is to make uncertainty visible, bounded, actionable, and governable.

---

## 2. Strategic Role in the Operating System

The Market-Intelligence Operating System is designed to produce disciplined judgments about ecosystems, participants, strategy, timing, risk, and entry vectors. Those judgments inevitably contain assumptions.

Some assumptions arise because:

- the market is partially opaque
- evidence is incomplete or contradictory
- future behavior cannot be directly observed yet
- strategic synthesis requires interpretation beyond descriptive fact
- platform or ecosystem conditions are changing faster than the evidence base can stabilize

The Assumption & Unknowns Register exists to prevent those realities from being buried inside elegant writing.

It serves six strategic roles:

1. **Boundary setting** — clarifies what the system knows versus what it believes.
2. **Risk exposure** — identifies which assumptions carry real downstream decision risk.
3. **Validation readiness** — creates a direct bridge into experiment design and concept testing.
4. **Decision hygiene** — prevents overconfident executive compression.
5. **Refresh discipline** — highlights which conclusions are most vulnerable to new information.
6. **Learning-loop integrity** — allows future outcomes to be compared against explicit prior assumptions.

This document is therefore a core governance instrument, not a narrative template.

---

## 3. What This Document Is

This document is:

- a structured register of assumptions embedded in analysis
- a governed inventory of unknowns and unresolved questions
- a severity map for uncertainty exposure
- a bridge between research and validation
- a mechanism for preventing hidden fragility in strategic outputs

---

## 4. What This Document Is Not

The Assumption & Unknowns Register is **not**:

- a place to excuse weak research
- a substitute for evidence collection
- a brainstorming dump of every possible question
- a narrative risk memo
- a prediction log without decision linkage
- a vague disclaimer layer

It does not justify avoidable ignorance. It governs unavoidable uncertainty.

---

## 5. When to Commission or Use It

The register should be created once a body of formal ecosystem research begins to mature and meaningful judgments are being formed.

It becomes mandatory whenever any of the following are true:

- Ecosystem Analysis conclusions are being drafted
- T1–T10 templates contain rankings, strategic judgments, or inferred dynamics
- T13 Executive Decision Memo is being prepared
- T14 Vector-to-Concept Conversion is being initiated
- T15 Validation & Experiment Design work is about to begin
- the work will influence capital, product, platform, or market-entry decisions

It should not be postponed until after a decision is made.

The register must exist before strategic compression is finalized.

---

## 6. Mandatory vs Conditional Use

**Mandatory** for:

- formal Ecosystem Analysis work
- all T1–T10 work that includes judgment, synthesis, or prioritization
- T13 Executive Decision Memo
- T14 Vector-to-Concept Conversion Brief
- T15 Validation & Experiment Design Standard
- T16 Refresh & Monitoring Protocol
- T18 Cross-Ecosystem Portfolio Scorecard
- T19 Learning Capture Memo

**Conditionally recommended** for:

- exploratory ecosystem scans
- early framing exercises
- low-stakes opportunity landscaping that is not yet decision-bearing

Where uncertainty may later influence a major decision, the register should be established early.

## 8. Outputs Produced

The register produces the following outputs:

- a governed inventory of assumptions
- explicit separation between knowns, beliefs, and unknowns
- severity-ranked uncertainty map
- decision-critical assumption set
- validation candidate queue
- unresolved-question registry
- refresh-sensitive conclusion flags
- historical record of what the system believed before outcomes arrived

These outputs support strategic honesty and downstream testing discipline.

---

## 9. Position in Sequencing Logic

T12 should be developed after evidence and analytical work begin to generate judgments, but before those judgments are compressed into final strategic recommendations.

Recommended sequence:

1. scope defined
2. T11 Evidence Ledger active
3. EA and selective T1–T10 work proceed
4. **T12 Assumption & Unknowns Register established and populated**
5. T13 Executive Decision Memo prepared
6. T10 vectors or strategic options reviewed against assumptions
7. T14 concept conversion initiated
8. T15 validation plan built from highest-risk assumptions
9. T16 and T19 update the register as the market evolves and outcomes arrive

T12 therefore sits between analysis and action, but also persists beyond both.

## 11. Core Analytical Questions

The register should allow a reviewer to answer the following questions quickly:

1. What assumptions does this body of work rely on?
2. Which assumptions are explicit and which were previously implicit?
3. Which assumptions are harmless and which are decision-critical?
4. Which assumptions exist because evidence is missing, contradictory, stale, or interpretive?
5. What is genuinely unknown rather than merely unverified?
6. Which assumptions can be tested, and how?
7. Which assumptions should block progress until resolved?
8. Which unknowns matter now versus later?
9. Which outputs are most exposed if an assumption fails?
10. What did the system believe at the time the decision was made?

## 12. Required Structure of the Register

The Assumption & Unknowns Register should contain the following core sections.

### 12.1 Header
- ecosystem or project name
- version number
- owner / maintainer
- date created
- last updated date
- decision horizon
- status

### 12.2 Assumption Registry
A structured list of all meaningful assumptions embedded in the analytical stack.

### 12.3 Unknowns Registry
A structured list of unresolved questions, blind spots, opaque areas, and unavailable knowledge.

### 12.4 Severity Map
A summary of which assumptions or unknowns carry the greatest downstream risk.

### 12.5 Validation Queue
A prioritized list of assumptions that should be tested through T15 or other evidence-gathering methods.

### 12.6 Blocking Conditions
A section for assumptions or unknowns severe enough to pause or constrain forward movement.

### 12.7 Change Log
A record of how assumptions, unknowns, and statuses evolve over time.

## 14. Assumption Type Framework

Each assumption must be categorized so the system understands what kind of fragility it is dealing with.

Recommended categories:

- **Behavioral assumption** — assumes participant behavior, motivation, adoption, or response
- **Structural assumption** — assumes something about ecosystem shape or force persistence
- **Strategic assumption** — assumes an entry vector, wedge, or positioning logic will hold
- **Trust assumption** — assumes credibility, legitimacy, or adoption thresholds can be crossed
- **Timing assumption** — assumes market timing, readiness, or rhythm conditions
- **Access assumption** — assumes platform access, approval, distribution, or operational feasibility
- **Monetization assumption** — assumes pricing power, willingness to pay, margin logic, or revenue path
- **Execution assumption** — assumes the organization can actually deliver on the requirement
- **Comparative assumption** — assumes one ecosystem, segment, or vector is stronger than another
- **Validation assumption** — assumes a test method is fit to reveal the truth needed

A single assumption may touch multiple domains, but one primary type should be assigned.

---

## 15. Unknowns Framework

Unknowns are not identical to assumptions.

An **assumption** is a proposition the analysis is currently leaning on.
An **unknown** is a gap or unresolved domain where the system does not yet know enough to make a stable claim.

Recommended unknown categories:

- missing data
- inaccessible evidence
- unresolved contradiction
- future-state ambiguity
- segment opacity
- operational blind spot
- platform policy uncertainty
- pricing uncertainty
- trust or adoption opacity
- competitive response uncertainty

The register must keep these distinct.

Unknowns become dangerous when quietly converted into assumptions without governance.

---

## 16. Why Assumptions Exist

Every assumption should record why it exists. Recommended reasons include:

- evidence incomplete
- evidence contradictory
- evidence stale
- evidence too costly to gather now
- future behavior cannot be observed directly
- inference required for strategic synthesis
- access limitation prevented confirmation
- platform or regulatory ambiguity remains unresolved

This matters because not all assumptions are equally blameworthy.
Some are unavoidable. Some are signs of weak process.

---

## 17. Decision Relevance Framework

Each assumption or unknown should be ranked by how directly it affects decisions.

Recommended categories:

- **Critical** — could materially change go / no-go, vector ranking, concept selection, or ecosystem choice
- **High** — unlikely to reverse the whole decision alone, but could materially distort execution or prioritization
- **Moderate** — meaningful, but manageable within adaptive execution
- **Low** — useful context, limited consequence if wrong

No executive decision memo should treat all assumptions as equally important.

---

## 18. Impact Area Framework

Each item should record which part of the system it threatens.

Recommended impact areas:

- ecosystem selection
- segment selection
- interface selection
- vector ranking
- concept translation
- validation design
- pricing / monetization
- trust acquisition
- platform access
- operational feasibility
- timing / launch window
- portfolio prioritization

This helps reveal where uncertainty is clustered.

---

## 19. Severity Framework

Severity should reflect the combined effect of:

- decision relevance
- blast radius
- time sensitivity
- difficulty of recovery if wrong
- closeness to irreversible commitment

Recommended severity classes:

- **S1 — Critical / blocking**
- **S2 — High / decision-relevant**
- **S3 — Moderate / manageable**
- **S4 — Low / monitor**

Severity is not the same as confidence.
A low-confidence assumption may still be low severity if little depends on it.

---

## 20. Blast Radius Framework

The register should record how much damage occurs if the assumption fails.

Recommended categories:

- local
- multi-section
- system-wide
- decision-reversing

This prevents underestimating assumptions that sit beneath multiple outputs.

---

## 21. Confidence Handling

The register should capture the confidence level associated with the assumption itself.

Recommended categories:

- high confidence assumption
- moderate confidence assumption
- provisional assumption
- weak assumption

This does not mean the assumption is true.
It reflects how strong the current basis is for leaning on it.

---

## 22. Testability Framework

Not every assumption can be resolved in the same way. The register should record testability.

Recommended categories:

- directly testable now
- testable with moderate effort
- testable only after concept formation
- observable only over time
- currently not testable

This is essential for building a clean handoff into T15.

---

## 23. Status Framework

Each assumption and unknown must have an explicit status.

Recommended statuses:

- identified
- under review
- accepted provisionally
- validation required
- actively being tested
- resolved
- retired
- superseded
- blocked pending resolution

The register must show movement over time.
A static assumption list is not governance.

---

## 24. Blocking Conditions Standard

An assumption or unknown should be considered **blocking** when any of the following are true:

- its failure would reverse a go / no-go decision
- it materially undermines the selected ecosystem or platform
- it invalidates the current highest-ranked entry vector
- it directly threatens platform access or legality
- it makes monetization logic too speculative to proceed responsibly
- it creates a trust hurdle that is likely prohibitive
- the organization has no credible path to validate it before commitment

Blocking items should be surfaced prominently in T13 and T14.

---

## 25. Unknowns Triage Standard

Unknowns should be triaged according to actionability.

### 25.1 Resolve Now
Unknowns that materially affect imminent decisions and can be addressed through additional research or direct inquiry.

### 25.2 Test Later
Unknowns that matter, but are best resolved through structured validation rather than further desk research.

### 25.3 Monitor
Unknowns that are real but not yet decision-bearing.

### 25.4 Accept
Unknowns that cannot be resolved economically at this stage and should be accepted with explicit boundaries.

This prevents the system from becoming either reckless or paralyzed.

---

## 26. Required Usage Rules

The following rules are mandatory.

### Rule 1 — No hidden assumptions
If a strategic judgment depends on an assumption, that assumption must be registered.

### Rule 2 — No false certainty
Unknowns must not be collapsed into declarative confidence for rhetorical convenience.

### Rule 3 — No equal treatment of unequal uncertainty
Decision-critical assumptions must be distinguished from minor uncertainties.

### Rule 4 — No validation planning without assumption visibility
T15 should not be produced without drawing from T12.

### Rule 5 — No executive memo without uncertainty disclosure
T13 must cite major critical assumptions and blocking conditions.

### Rule 6 — No stale assumptions preserved by inertia
Assumptions must be revisited when evidence changes, markets shift, or experiments fail.

### Rule 7 — No assumption laundering through scoring systems
If a portfolio score, vector rank, or platform attractiveness rating depends on assumptions, the register must reveal that dependency.

---

## 27. Recommended Operating Procedure

### Step 1 — Extract assumptions from the analytical stack
Review EA and T1–T10 for hidden dependencies, strategic claims, and future-facing judgments.

### Step 2 — Separate assumptions from unknowns
Do not treat unresolved questions and active beliefs as the same thing.

### Step 3 — Classify and severity-rank each item
Assign type, relevance, impact area, severity, and blast radius.

### Step 4 — Identify blocking items
Flag assumptions or unknowns that should constrain forward movement.

### Step 5 — Build the validation queue
Move the highest-risk, most testable assumptions into a structured sequence for T15.

### Step 6 — Use the register in executive compression
Ensure T13 reflects critical assumptions honestly.

### Step 7 — Update after experiments or new evidence
As evidence improves, contradictions resolve, or validation occurs, revise the register.

### Step 8 — Feed outcomes into learning capture
When reality arrives, compare it against what the system previously assumed.

---

## 28. Quality Standard / Acceptance Criteria

The Assumption & Unknowns Register should be considered acceptable only if all of the following are true:

1. Major strategic judgments have their key assumptions surfaced explicitly.
2. Assumptions and unknowns are kept distinct.
3. Each item is classified by type, relevance, severity, and status.
4. Decision-critical and blocking items are visible.
5. The register can directly inform T13 and T15.
6. The system can identify which outputs are most exposed to assumption failure.
7. The register is updated when evidence or outcomes change.
8. A reviewer can see what the operating system knew, did not know, and was choosing to believe at the time of decision.

---

## 29. Failure Modes and Misuse Warnings

Common failure modes include:

### 29.1 Disclaimer theater
A vague note that “some assumptions exist” is not governance.

### 29.2 Overpopulation
If every minor interpretive nuance becomes an entry, the register loses signal.

### 29.3 Underpopulation
If only obvious assumptions are logged, hidden fragility remains embedded in the analysis.

### 29.4 Confusing uncertainty with weakness
A visible assumption register strengthens judgment. It does not weaken it.

### 29.5 Failing to distinguish testable from non-testable assumptions
Validation efforts become wasteful when this distinction is ignored.

### 29.6 Static treatment
An old assumption register becomes a historical artifact instead of a live control system.

### 29.7 Executive sanitization
If critical assumptions are stripped out when findings are summarized for decision-makers, the system fails at the point of highest consequence.

---

## 30. Source and Evidence Expectations

The register should draw from T11 rather than freehand opinion.

Each assumption should, where possible, reference:

- the evidence condition that gave rise to it
- contradictions or evidence gaps connected to it
- the outputs affected by it
- the best available path to strengthen, validate, or retire it

The register must not become a substitute for the Evidence Ledger.
It is a governance companion to the ledger, not a replacement.

## 32. Relationship to Agentic Research Systems

The Assumption & Unknowns Register should be designed for both human and agentic use.

This means:

- stable IDs for assumptions and unknowns
- discrete enum-like fields where practical
- explicit linkage to T11 source and contradiction records
- clear status transitions
- direct machine-legible handoff into T15 and T19

An agent should be able to extract candidate assumptions from analytical text, but a reviewer must still govern severity, relevance, and blocking status.

The register should support semi-automated detection without surrendering judgment.

---

## 33. Versioning Notes

The Assumption & Unknowns Register Standard should evolve when any of the following occur:

- repeated work reveals recurring hidden assumptions that the framework does not capture well
- validation programs show that testability categories are too crude
- refresh cycles reveal that time sensitivity needs stronger handling
- portfolio comparison repeatedly fails to account for uncertainty burden
- post-launch learning shows that failure consequences were poorly modeled
- platform ecosystem work introduces new forms of access or policy uncertainty that deserve explicit treatment

Version updates should record which governance weakness or pattern prompted revision.

---

## 34. Final Standard Statement

A strong market-intelligence system does not pretend to know more than it knows.

The Assumption & Unknowns Register exists to make the system’s uncertainty legible, ranked, testable, and governable.

Without T12, the operating system can still sound confident.
With T12, it can become trustworthy under uncertainty.
